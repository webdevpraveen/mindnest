# MindNest — Student Wellness Community

> **College Project (MVP)** — Built as part of the L&T EduTech initiative to address rising mental health concerns among Indian college students.

---

##  What is MindNest?

MindNest is a peer-to-peer wellness platform designed exclusively for college students. It provides a safe, anonymous, and supportive digital space where students can share what they're going through — academic pressure, exam stress, social anxiety, family expectations — without fear of judgment.

Unlike generic mental health apps, MindNest is built around the **Indian college experience**. It understands the unique struggles of CGPA pressure, attendance rules, placement anxiety, and the constant comparison on class WhatsApp groups.

---

##  Problem Statement

Indian college students face a silent mental health crisis:

- **77%** of students report exam-related anxiety (NIMHANS, 2023)
- Most students **never seek professional help** due to stigma, cost, or lack of awareness
- Existing platforms feel clinical and disconnected from real student life
- Students often suffer in silence because they think **"no one else feels this way"**

There is no safe, relatable, student-first space where they can express themselves freely and find peer support.

---

##  How MindNest Solves This

MindNest creates a **community-driven support system** where students help each other through daily check-ins, anonymous venting, and positive reinforcement:

### 1. Daily Mood Check-in
Students log how they're feeling each day. This builds self-awareness and helps the community understand collective stress patterns (e.g., exam weeks show higher stress across the board).

### 2. Community Feed
A safe social feed where students can share wins, worries, tips, and experiences. Posts can be made **anonymously** or with identity — the choice is always with the student.

### 3. Vent Room
A dedicated anonymous space to let it all out. Every vent receives validation from peers ("I feel you too") instead of unsolicited advice. Students can also send anonymous comfort DMs or flag severe cases for a counselor.

### 4. Breathe & Reset
Guided breathing exercises (4-7-8 technique, Box Breathing, Quick Calm) to help students manage anxiety in real-time — right before an exam, during a panic episode, or before sleeping.

### 5. Private Journal
A personal, private space to write thoughts without any audience. Journaling is proven to reduce anxiety, and MindNest makes it easy with mood tagging and daily prompts.

### 6. Wellness Points & Rewards
Students earn points for positive actions — checking in daily, validating someone's vent, writing journal entries, completing breathing sessions. Points can be redeemed for real rewards (café vouchers, book discounts, streaming subscriptions), making wellness feel rewarding instead of like a chore.

### 7. Peer Messaging
Direct, private conversations between students who connect through the community. Built for genuine support, not social media noise.

---

##  How Students Use MindNest

1. **Open the app** → Land on the Community Feed where you can see what fellow students are sharing
2. **Check in your mood** → Select how you're feeling today (Great / Good / Okay / Low / Rough)
3. **Share or vent** → Post on the feed or head to the Vent Room for anonymous expression
4. **Support others** → Validate someone's vent, send a comfort DM, like and react to posts
5. **Breathe** → Use guided breathing when anxiety hits
6. **Journal** → Write your thoughts privately
7. **Earn rewards** → Every positive action earns wellness points that unlock real rewards
